Brand Name

The brand name is OutnCenter. It is a play on Front Center but using Out instead of Front for LGBTQ+ visibility.

Brand vision

In the world of theatre where LGBTQ+ characters are often misrepresented, we believe in shining spotlight on authentic storytelling. Because everyone at centre stage deserves to be out and proud.

Values

  • Authenticity - We are committed to authenticity, and proudly celebrating LGBTQ+ voices in theatre.
  • Learning - Engaging with our brand means always discovering something new.
  • Inclusivity- We welcome all who support and engage with Lgbtq+ theatre.

Problem

  • All the information is at different places which makes it very unorganized.
  • There is a lot of misinterpretation in theatre about the LGBTQ+ community.
  • They want to read articles about misrepresentation but it is hard to find reliable sources and to find interesting articles.
  • They want to easily find out about different queer plays that are playing in their area.

Solution

  • A website where you can see all the shows playing with dates, theatres and reviews aout a show where you can immediately buy a ticket to a show. It is easy, accessible and organized.
  • A platform where individuals can engage in discussions about different topics in theatre.
  • A platform where individuals can easily find reliable articles that are discussing topics such as misrepresentation of the Lgbtq+ community in theatre.

Customer Segments

  • Adults 20 to 35 years old
  • Based in Europe
  • Attend the theatre 2/3 times per year
  • Enjoy a mix of performances and are often seeking productions that challenge societal norms
  • Favour theatre productions that are progressive and inclusive of diverse identities, experiences and stories
  • Follow theatre companies, actors online especially those who champion LGBTQ+ narratives and themes
  • Engage with theatre-related content online
  • They are part of the LGBTQ+ community or are allies

We stand out as an online platform that is dedicated to create a safe space for theater lovers to actively explore, discuss and celebrate LGBTQ+ representation in theater. We focus entirely on amplifying queer voices, fostering critical conversations and making representation more accessibler and visible.

  • A website as an online platform
  • Safe space for the community
  • Creating a fun and safe space for the theater community to discuss and discover new plays and to raise awareness about current representation issues

Cost Structure

Fixed Costs

  • Hosting – The cost of keeping the website live and accessible
  • People – Salaries to employees and contributors
  • Ticket management system – The system is developed/licensed for a fixed fee rather than per transaction

Variable Costs

  • Customer acquisition costs – All the expenses related to sales, marketing, and other activities aimed at attracting and converting potential customers into paying ones
  • Distributing costs – Costs will be made for the distribution of the brand and website
  • Marketing costs – The costs that go towards the marketing campaign to promote the brand

Revenue Streams

  • Advertisement based - On our website advertisment space is sold and from this income is generated
  • Freemium Revenue Model (Subscription option for 2,99 euro/month) - In order to read certain articles individuals will be asked to pay a certain subscription as well to buy certain tickets to plays
  • Affiliate marketing – Theatre events and shows

Problem Interview Conclusions

Based on the interviews that were done with theatre actors and theatre lovers the following results were found:

Theatre actors

  • Need better access to auditions, industry connections, and mentors
  • Struggle with financial stability; balance acting with side jobs
  • Desire a supportive community, particularly one that celebrates diversity and inclusivity
  • Want rehearsal spaces that nurture growth, creativity, and failure without judgment
  • Desire more LGBTQ+ representation in theatre
  • Need career planning tools and sustainable income strategies.

Theatre lovers

  • Struggle to discover shows and are looking for a centralized platform with all the information about shows
  • Prefer musicals, high production value, and immersive storytelling.
  • Have a desire for more inclusive storytelling
  • Are frustrated by superficial themes and stereotypical characters in theatre especially LGBTQ+ characters
  • Rely on word-of-mouth, social media, and email marketing for show discovery
  • Enjoy behind-the-scenes content like cast interviews and rehearsals
  • Go to the theatre 2–3 times per year especially when they are traveling
  • Want better ticketing systems
  • Seek a platform that connects them with others who share their passion for inclusive theatre

Our Brand

Out n Center is an inclusive theatre brand that strives for more realistic LGBTQ+ representation in theatre.

Our platform provides informative articles about LGBTQ+ representation awareness and insightful interviews with professionals from the industry. Moreover, we provide a ticketing service where visitors can gain information about new plays and immediately are directed to theatre pages for tickets.

Target audience

The target audience consists of young to middle-aged adults who identify as a part of the LGBTQ+ community or are strong allies. They are deeply passionate about theatre, seeing it not just as entertainment but as a powerful medium for artistic expressions, storytelling, and authentic representation. Their engagement goes beyond casual attendance as they actively participate in theatre culture both online and in person. Digitally savvy and socially conscious, they are eager to support productions that reflect their values. For them theatre is not only a source of enjoyment but also a meaningful space for identity, inclusion and connection.

Key Characteristics

  • 20-35 years old
  • Primarily based in Europe, with a strong presence in urban areas where theatre culture thrives.
  • LGBTQ+ individuals and allies who support inclusive and diverse narratives in the arts.
  • Interests and values: Inclusivity, creativity, storytelling, activism, and cultural engagement. They often seek out stories that reflect diverse experiences, particularly those related to the LGBTQ+ community.
  • Attend the theatre 2/3 times per year.
  • Enjoy a mix of performances and are often seeking productions that challenge societal norms.
  • Favour theatre productions that are progressive and inclusive of diverse identities, experiences and stories.
  • Follow theatre companies, actors online especially those who champion LGBTQ+ narratives and themes.
  • Engage with theatre-related content online.
  • Active on platforms like Instagram, TikTok, YouTube.
  • Use social media to discover new plays.
  • Find new theatre experiences through word of mouth and social media.

Reach Objectives

Marketing campaign

Context of the campaign

Campaign objective

This campaign aims to foster a more accepting environment for LGBTQ+ characters in theatre, film, and television by advocating for authentic representation.

Misrepresentation, whether through stereotypes, one-dimensional portrayals, or tokenism, does not just fail the community, it actively harms it. Our goal is to raise awareness about the impact of problematic representation and encourage creators to craft LGBTQ+ characters that feel real, complex, and human.

Key Message

To communicate why authentic representation matters, a series of strong, concise statements will be de developing that will highlight both the importance of real representation and the harm of poor representation.

  • LGBTQ+ characters deserve depth, not stereotypes.
  • Sexuality isn’t a personality!
  • Representation shapes reality. When we reduce characters, we reduce people.
  • Visibility should empower, not diminish. Authenticity matters.

Call to Action

The call to action is to redefine LGBTQ+ representation in theatre. Share, discuss, and demand stories that reflect the full depth of human experience, because real representation changes lives. (might need to be adjusted)
